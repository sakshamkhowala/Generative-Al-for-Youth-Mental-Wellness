Crisis Action Plan

  1. Get to a safe place - Remove yourself from immediate danger
  2. Call for help - Use the numbers above or call emergency services
  3. Stay with someone - Don't be alone if possible
  4. Remove harmful objects - Put away anything that could cause harm
  5. Use coping strategies - Try breathing exercises or grounding techniques
  6. Follow up with professional help - Make an appointment as soon as possible

Warning Signs to Watch For

  • Persistent sadness lasting more than 2 weeks
  • Anxiety interfering with daily activities
  • Thoughts of self-harm or suicide
  • Significant changes in sleep, appetite, or behavior
  • Substance use as coping mechanism
  • Difficulty functioning at school or in relationships

Coping Strategies

Breathing Exercises

Box Breathing

Breathe in for 4, hold for 4, exhale for 4, hold for 4

  1. Breathe in through your nose for 4 counts
  2. Hold your breath for 4 counts
  3. Exhale through your mouth for 4 counts
  4. Hold empty for 4 counts
  5. Repeat 5-10 times

Belly Breathing

Deep breathing focusing on stomach expansion

  1. Place one hand on your chest, one on your belly
  2. Breathe in slowly through your nose
  3. Feel your belly rise more than your chest
  4. Exhale slowly through pursed lips
  5. Practice for 5-10 minutes

5-Finger Breathing

Trace your hand while breathing

  1. Hold up one hand in front of you
  2. Use other finger to trace up thumb while inhaling
  3. Trace down thumb while exhaling
  4. Continue for all 5 fingers
  5. Repeat as needed

Body Scan

Mentally check in with each part of your body

  1. Start with your toes and feet
  2. Notice any tension or sensation
  3. Move up through legs, torso, arms
  4. End with your head and face
  5. Just observe without judgment

Mindful Listening

Focus on sounds around you

  1. Sit comfortably and close your eyes
  2. Listen to all sounds around you
  3. Don't label them, just notice
  4. If your mind wanders, return to listening

Professional Help

Understanding when and how to seek professional mental health support

When to Seek Professional Help

It's time to reach out to a professional if you're experiencing:

  • Persistent sadness lasting more than 2 weeks
  • Anxiety interfering with daily activities
  • Thoughts of self-harm or suicide
  • Significant changes in sleep, appetite, or behavior
  • Substance use as a coping mechanism
  • Difficulty functioning at school or in relationships
Remember: Seeking help is a sign of strength, not weakness. Early intervention leads to better outcomes.

Talking to Family About Mental Health

Tips for Starting the Conversation:

  • Choose a calm, private moment
  • Be honest about how you're feeling
  • Explain that mental health is just as important as physical health
  • Share specific examples of how you've been affected
  • Ask for their support in finding help
  • Be patient - they may need time to understand

Example conversation starter:

"I've been struggling with my mental health lately, and I think I could benefit from talking to someone professional. Can we look into finding a counselor or therapist together?"
